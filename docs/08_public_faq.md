# Public FAQ

## 1) What is Solar Oracle Walkman?
Solar Oracle Walkman is a public research prototype that links DSSC measurement, IV voiceprint features, oracle signatures, and blockchain records.

## 2) What is a DSSC?
A DSSC is a dye-sensitized solar cell; in this project it is also explored as a possible physical co-factor.

## 3) What is an I–V curve?
An I–V curve shows how current responds to voltage during a measurement.

## 4) What is an IV voiceprint?
It is a compact feature vector derived from I–V data and used as a candidate material identity signal.

## 5) What does the current V1 prototype do?
It validates a 7-value IV data vector, verifies oracle signatures, and stores accepted records on-chain.

## 6) What is the smart contract for?
It acts as a verification and record layer for signed IV measurement reports.

## 7) Is this a legal REC or T-REC system?
No. This repository is not a legal REC or T-REC registry.

## 8) Is this a financial product?
No. It is not a financial product.

## 9) Does DSSC replace TPM?
No. DSSC is not presented as a replacement for TPM or secure elements.

## 10) What is a material event signature?
A material event signature is evidence of a measured physical response under a defined challenge and time context.

## 11) What does "energy provenance evidence" mean?
It means research evidence that connects measured physical events to verifiable records.

## 12) What is the current project status?
V1 smart contract exists; broader registry, characterization, and public explainer layers are in progress.

## 13) What should technical advisors review first?
Review feature quality, measurement protocol, environmental controls, and claims boundaries.

## 14) What is the difference between art prototype and security product?
An art/research prototype explores ideas and evidence workflows; a security product requires robust validation, hardening, and audit.

## 15) How can collaborators help?
Contribute measurement protocols, dataset quality controls, schema improvements, documentation, and critical review.
